The Science of Emotion: Exploring
The Basics of Emotional Psychology

How we interpret and respond to the world around us makes up who we are and contributes to our quality of life. The study of emotional psychology1 allows researchers to dive into what makes humans react as they do to certain stimuli and how those reactions2 affect us both physically3 and mentally4. While the study of emotional psychology is vast and complex, researchers have discovered quite a bit about what constitutes our emotions and our behavioral5 and physical reactions to them.

1. Emotional psychology: how we interpret and respond to the world around us makes up who we are and contributes to our quality of life.
2. Reaction: an action performed or a feeling experienced in response to a situation or event.
3. Physical: relating to the body as opposed to the mind.
4. Mental: relating to the mind.
5. Behaviour: the way in which one acts or conducts oneself, toward others.
