Quick Notes

Emotional psychology explores how we react to stimuli and its effects on our well-being. Emotions shape our identity and influence our quality of life.

Chapter 1 – Defining Emotions Emotions involve three components: subjective experience, physiological response, and behavioral response. Feelings arise from emotions and are influenced by memories & beliefs. Moods are low-intensity emotional states without a clear trigger. Emotional definitions are still evolving, with ongoing research and theories.

Chapter 2 – Emotions and Psychology Basic emotions are universal and linked to facial expressions.
Paul Ekman’s six basic emotions: Happiness, sadness, fear, anger, surprise, disgust. Later additions: Pride, shame, embarrassment, amusement, etc. Emotions are essential for survival and social interaction.

Chapter 3 – Benefits of Understanding Emotions Emotions guide our behavior and influence decision-making.
Emotional intelligence (EI): Recognizing, managing, and understanding emotions in ourselves and others. EI helps in relationships, careers, and overall well-being. EI can be improved over time through awareness and practice.
